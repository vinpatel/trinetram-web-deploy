Capabilities in the TriNetram concept platform

Two hundred and fifty years ago, Benjamin Franklin cut a bifocal lens in half and glued the halves together. Most of the world still wears the same idea.
Four and a half billion people globally need vision correction. The lens in front of their eyes is a static piece of polymer ground to one specific prescription, measured in a clinic, worn until the next visit.
Vision changes by the hour. Light changes by the second. The lens does not change at all.

The TriNetram concept platform is designed to close that gap — adaptive prescription, on-device AI, dual-camera focus, face recognition, solar occlusion, and IR low-light awareness in one frame. Concept Preview.

The Six Capabilities

Six capabilities. One frame.

No single capability defines the TriNetram concept platform. The thesis is the integrated bundle — six subsystems that share a single AI compute core, a shared power envelope, and a shared frame. Each capability sits at Concept readiness as of today; Phase 4 selects subsystem suppliers. Concept Preview.

01

Concept

Adaptive Lens

Adaptive prescription correction is the foundational capability of the TriNetram concept platform.

An adaptive lens designed to change its optical power on command, across the range commonly needed for distance and reading correction. Our concept architecture targets a diopter range of approximately -6.00 D to +3.00 D. In our reference design, the lens element holds a diopter without continuous power draw. Concept Preview.

A dual-camera dynamic focus engine — our distinct mechanism for adaptive prescription. Concept Preview.

Two inward-facing precision cameras designed to measure pupil state and accommodation effort in real time within the concept architecture. Measured at 60–120 frames per second in the concept design. Designed to infer the wearer's intended focus distance from accommodation patterns. Concept Preview.

Why Now

Four currents converge.

Adaptive prescription eyewear has been a research curiosity for fifteen years. In 2026 four independent technology curves cross. Each is necessary. None alone is sufficient.

01

AI silicon at sub-watt power.

Neural processing units that ran in datacenter racks five years ago now fit in a hearing-aid envelope at a fraction of the power budget. The compute to run prescription inference, face recognition, and scene understanding on a temple-arm PCB has finally arrived. Concept Preview.

02

Variable-focus optics reach consumer grade.

Liquid-crystal and electrowetting lens elements have shipped in research and industrial products for more than a decade. The price-to-performance curve crossed the consumer eyewear threshold in 2025. Multiple subsystem vendors are now sampling at glasses scale. Concept Preview.

03

Computer-vision inference below 5 milliseconds.

A learned model that estimates accommodation effort from a corneal-reflection frame now runs in single-digit milliseconds on commodity edge silicon. The closed pupil-tracker to lens-driver loop the adaptive-vision concept depends on is no longer compute-bound. Concept Preview.

04

Consumers wear AI cameras on their face.

AI-camera eyewear shipped over two million units in eighteen months after the consumer breakout of 2023. The cultural objection to a camera on the frame collapsed; the remaining objection is that the lens itself is still inert.

The platform that integrates adaptive prescription with on-device AI inference, dual-camera tracking, face recognition, solar occlusion, and IR low-light awareness is a question of execution, not of waiting. Concept Preview.

Questions

Frequently asked.

What is TriNetram?
TriNetram is a concept platform for AI-native adaptive vision. The integrated frame design combines adaptive prescription, on-device AI inference, dual-camera dynamic focus, face recognition, solar occlusion, and IR low-light awareness. Concept Preview — hardware is in research phase.
When will TriNetram glasses ship?
We do not have a ship date and we will not commit to one before a manufacturing-partner letter of intent is signed. The waitlist opens at the first prototype validation milestone; you will be notified at that point.
Is this FDA approved or clinically validated?
No. TriNetram is not a medical device. The platform is designed for wellness and adaptive convenience, not for clinical refraction or diagnosis. Regulatory positioning is the founder's separate workstream and is out of scope for the current build.
How is this different from existing autofocus or smart glasses?
The defensible thesis is the integrated bundle, not primacy. Variable-focus eyewear has shipped for over a decade as manual-dial products. AI-camera glasses have shipped since 2023. No vendor has integrated adaptive prescription with on-device AI inference, dual-camera dynamic focus, face recognition, solar occlusion, and IR low-light awareness in a single frame. Concept Preview.
Who is building this?
TriNetram is a single-founder operation today. The founder has 25 years of enterprise software and the last decade close to optical, sensor, and edge-compute industries. Hardware, supplier, and regulatory tracks are run with named advisors and contract partners under NDA. The investor portal discloses the full roster.
How do I get the deck?
Investor access is by per-investor magic link. We do not run an open data room. Targeted outreach with a magic link reaches a gated session at /investors. Press inquiries reach the same human at press@trinetram.ai.
Where is it being made?
Designed in the US, with final assembly planned in the US or Canada. Subsystem suppliers may come from Japan, Germany, Switzerland, Israel, Taiwan, and South Korea — selected for world-class capability in their respective subsystems, not for protectionist branding.

In the concept architecture, prescription inference runs on the device — your prescription data does not leave the glasses without your consent. The companion app receives encrypted telemetry over BLE 5.4 only when paired. Concept Preview.
